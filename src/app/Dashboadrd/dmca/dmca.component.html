Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA):

The Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA) is a United States copyright law enacted in 1998. It addresses the rights and responsibilities of online service providers (OSPs) and copyright owners regarding copyrighted material posted on the internet. The DMCA provides a framework for copyright owners to request the removal of infringing content from websites or online platforms.

Website owners and online service providers can benefit from certain safe harbor provisions under the DMCA if they promptly remove infringing content upon receiving a valid DMCA takedown notice. The DMCA also includes provisions for counter-notices, allowing users who believe their content was wrongly removed to contest the takedown.

General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a European Union regulation implemented in 2018. It aims to protect the privacy and personal data of EU citizens. The GDPR applies to organizations that collect, process, or store personal data of EU residents, regardless of the organization's location.

Key principles of the GDPR include transparency, consent, data minimization, and the rights of individuals to access, rectify, and erase their personal data. Organizations must implement measures to ensure the security and lawful processing of personal data, and they may face significant fines for non-compliance with the GDPR.

California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA):

The California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA) is a privacy law passed in November 2020, amending and expanding upon the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

The CPRA enhances privacy protections for California residents by introducing new requirements for businesses handling personal information.

Key provisions of the CPRA include the establishment of a new enforcement agency (California Privacy Protection Agency), expanded rights for consumers, such as the right to limit the use of sensitive personal information, and increased obligations for businesses regarding data protection measures and transparency.

The CPRA imposes additional requirements on businesses that process large volumes of data, engage in profiling or automated decision-making, or handle sensitive personal information.
